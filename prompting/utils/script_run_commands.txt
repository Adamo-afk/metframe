Apply stopword removal ONLY to token-based metrics. This is the important design decision. Stopword removal is right for ROUGE, BLEU, METEOR, and Jaccard — all four are n-gram-based and stopwords dominate the overlap (articles like "a", "cel", "de" appear in nearly every sentence and inflate scores without measuring anything meaningful about meteorological content).
But stopword removal is wrong for BERTScore. BERTScore computes contextual embeddings over the full text; stopwords provide grammatical scaffolding that the encoder uses to produce meaningful vectors for the content words around them. Stripping stopwords before BERTScore degrades the contextual signal and makes scores less interpretable, not more. The bert-score paper explicitly recommends keeping full text for this reason.
